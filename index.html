I left the Navy with an interest in satellite imagery and programming. I was excited to be accepted to the Earth Sciences department and to join the remote sensing laboratory. I dove into IDL programming and using ENVI software. I also started to teach myself Python at this time and how to use it for ArcGIS scripts, image processing and many other tasks.

My thesis project was to write software to effectively use a car-mounted panoramic camera (Ladybug3 Spherical Camera) in analyzing landslide imagery. The goal was to use GPS and structure-from-motion (SFM) techniques to estimate landslide scale from video images and to process multiple videos to make a time-series of landslide imagery.
